<p><p>The travel sickness drugs market comprises several categories, primarily anticholinergics and antihistamines. Anticholinergic drugs, like scopolamine, work by blocking signals in the brain that induce nausea and vomiting, often used in patches for long journeys. Antihistamines, such as dimenhydrinate and meclizine, help alleviate motion sickness by addressing the body's histamine response. Additionally, the market includes other remedies like herbal supplements and homeopathic solutions, catering to diverse consumer preferences for managing travel-related discomfort.</p></p>
