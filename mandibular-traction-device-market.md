<p><p>The Mandibular Traction Device market is categorized into two main types: External Fixed Traction Devices and Internal Fixed Traction Devices. External Fixed Traction Devices operate outside the oral cavity, using frameworks attached to the skull or face to apply force on the mandible. In contrast, Internal Fixed Traction Devices are surgically implanted within the jaw, providing a more discreet and stable means of correcting mandibular positioning. Both types aim to enhance orthopedic treatment and improve jaw alignment and function.</p></p>
