<p><p>The Atmospheric Pressure Sintered Silicon Carbide (SiC) market includes two primary types: solid phase sintering and liquid phase sintering. Solid phase sintering involves densifying SiC at elevated temperatures without a liquid phase, resulting in strong, thermally stable materials. In contrast, liquid phase sintering incorporates a small amount of liquid phase to facilitate particle bonding, allowing for improved densification and enhanced mechanical properties. Both methods cater to diverse applications in industries like automotive, aerospace, and electronics, maximizing performance and durability.</p></p>
